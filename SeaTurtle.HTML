About Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are reptiles that live in the ocean and on beaches around the world. They can be found in warm and temperate waters, and their diet consists mainly of seagrass and algae. Sea turtles are known for their long lifespan, with some species living over 100 years.

Species of Sea Turtles

There are seven species of sea turtles:

  1. Green turtle
  2. Hawksbill turtle
  3. Loggerhead turtle
  4. Olive Ridley turtle
  5. Kemp's Ridley turtle
  6. Flatback turtle
  7. Leatherback turtle

Conservation of Sea Turtles

Sea turtles face many threats, including habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and poaching. Several conservation efforts are underway to protect sea turtles and their habitats. Some of these efforts include:

  • Protecting nesting beaches
  • Reducing plastic pollution in the ocean
  • Supporting sustainable fishing practices
  • Implementing laws and regulations to prevent poaching and trade of sea turtles and their products
